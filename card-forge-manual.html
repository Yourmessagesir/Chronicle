What Is Card Forge?

Card Forge is a self-contained tabletop game lab that lives in three HTML files. You design cards, set rules, draw board layouts, and then immediately play your game â€” all without any server, account, or internet connection after the first load.

Everything you create is saved to your browser's local storage and stays on your device. You can back it up, share it, and restore it at any time using the Export/Import tools.

The system was built to handle two distinct use cases:

Custom card games â€” build your own cards with stats, keywords, and multi-effect attacks. Define the rules, win conditions, resource system, and phase structure. Design a board layout, then play against an AI or pass the device between players.

Board game simulation â€” use the layout builder to draw a board, place interactive zones and markers, then use the playtest mode's dice roller, draggable pieces, and counters to run any physical board game digitally. Think Monopoly, Betrayal at House on the Hill, Clue, Ticket to Ride.

Installing as an App (PWA)

Both Card Forge and the companion app Chronicle can be installed as Progressive Web Apps â€” they appear on your home screen and work fully offline.

On iOS (Safari): Open the file, tap the Share icon, then "Add to Home Screen." You get two separate apps: Card Forge (forge.html) and Chronicle (lifeapp.html).

On Android (Chrome): Open the file, tap the three-dot menu, then "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App."

On Desktop (Chrome/Edge): Look for the install icon in the address bar (a computer with a down arrow). Click it to install.

Once installed, the app caches itself and runs completely offline. When you update the HTML files and reload, a banner appears offering to reload to the latest version.

Playtest â€” Overview

Playtest is the game table. It loads your Game Config (card game rules) and/or Layout (board), shuffles decks, and lets you play. It can also run as a pure board game with no cards at all.

Playing a Card Game

Select a Game Config from the setup screen. Decks are automatically built from all cards linked to that config (or all cards if none are linked), shuffled, and dealt. You are Player 1; the AI plays as Player 2.

The Game Screen

The header shows the current phase indicators and whose turn it is. The NEXT PHASE â–¶ button advances through your turn's phases. END TURN â­ skips to the end and passes to the opponent. ğŸ² DICE opens the dice roller.

Basic Turn Flow

Draw Phase: A card is drawn automatically. Resources refresh.

Main Phase: Tap a card in your hand to select it, then tap an empty battlefield slot to play it. Or tap it in the Card tab and hit Play to Field.

Combat Phase: Tap a card on your field to select it (it highlights in gold). Then tap an enemy card to attack it, or use Attack Directly from the Card tab to hit the player.

End Phase: Hit End Turn. The AI takes its turn, then your Draw Phase begins again.

Grid / Traverse Mode

In grid modes, select a card from your hand, then tap a grid cell in your half of the board to place it. Select a placed card and tap an adjacent cell to move, or an adjacent enemy card to attack.

In Traverse mode, item tiles scattered across the map give bonuses when a Collector card lands on them. Attack the enemy Crystal (shown as ğŸ”®) when you're adjacent to it.

Lane Battle Mode

Select a card from hand, then tap one of the three lane zones (Left, Center, Right) to place it. Tap an occupied enemy lane to attack. Win two lanes to win the game.

Co-op Combo Mode

Both players tap cards from their hands onto the shared Combo Stack. Each card's stats add to the stack. Build up the score to hit the threshold and win.

AI Opponent

The AI automatically takes its turn after you end yours. It plays cards from its hand based on the AI Style set in the Game Config:

Random â€” plays cards in random order.

Difficulty affects how many cards the AI plays per turn: Easy (max 2), Medium (max 3), Hard (max 5). The AI also applies all keyword rules correctly â€” it respects Taunt, Flying, Defender, Frozen cards, etc.
